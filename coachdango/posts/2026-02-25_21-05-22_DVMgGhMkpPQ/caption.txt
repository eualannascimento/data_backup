My client was doing everything “right.”

10,000 steps daily. ✓
Gym 7 days a week. ✓
Eating “healthy.” ✓

Yet, still gaining weight.

“I don’t understand,” he said. “I’m killing myself.”

I had him track his food for 3 days.

The truth:

- Morning smoothie: 600 calories
- “Healthy” lunch salad: 850 calories (dressing!)
- Post-workout shake: 400 calories
- “Small” dinner: 1,200 calories
- Random snacks: 500 calories

Total: 3,550 calories
His maintenance: 2,400 calories

He was eating 1,150 extra calories daily.

That’s 8,050 calories per week.
Over 2 lbs of fat gain.

While walking 70,000 steps.
While hitting the gym daily.

You can’t out-train your fork or out-walk your mouth.

Physics doesn’t care about your effort. Only your deficit.

So we cut him to 2,200 calories. Kept everything else the same.

8 weeks later: Down 16 lbs.

Same steps. Same workouts.
Different scale.

The uncomfortable truth:

If you’re not losing weight, you’re eating too much.

Period.
