As a kid, I was always insecure about my body.

I’d wear baggy clothes to hide it from people. I’d shy away from changing in front of other people.

Heck, I even wore t-shirts while swimming in the pool.

I was active and played a lot of sports, but my body kept getting fatter and fatter.

It was a thorn that had stuck in my side from grade school through my early 20s.

My parents were working all the time. They didn’t have the time most nights to cook a meal, so we ate McDonald’s and other forms of take-out.

(I could crush 2 Big Macs, super-sized fries + Coke and still be hungry for more)

During that time, I developed some horrible eating habits, and looking back, I was using food to numb many of the emotions I was feeling.

My body was the greatest source of my shame.

But then I found the gym.

The gym became my sanctuary. I became obsessed with learning about how the body functioned and, later on, about how to burn fat.

After changing my body, I became a personal trainer and then owned a gym for 11 years.

Since then, I’ve grown both as a person and professionally. I’ve achieved so many personal, financial, and spiritual goals.

And it’s because of the power of fitness.

My hope is that you learn from my lessons and use fitness as a conduit to make your life better.

If you give it a chance, transforming your body can transform your life.
