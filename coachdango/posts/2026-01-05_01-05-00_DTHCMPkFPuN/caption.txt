Stop snacking on autopilot.

Most cravings aren’t hunger. They’re stress, boredom, or dehydration wearing a disguise.

Try the 10-minute rule:

When a craving hits, pause. Take slow, deep breaths. Nose in, mouth out. This calms your nervous system and kills impulsivity.

Drink a full glass of water.

Set a timer for 10 minutes. Stay busy.

That’s it.

When the timer goes off, check in. Still hungry? Eat.

But most people find the urge is gone.

Because it was never real hunger. It was a habit pretending to be one.

10 minutes separates craving from control.
