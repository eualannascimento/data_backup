Ps. Correlation vs. causation is always worth questioning.

What’s interesting about this study is that it adjusted for key confounding variables like age, BMI, cardiovascular risk, and physical activity. 

Even after that, men who had sex less than once a week had significantly higher rates of disease over time.

In other words, it’s not just that healthy men have more sex, it’s that lower sexual activity itself is linked to higher disease risk, even when you account for overall health.

There’s likely a bidirectional relationship here: less sex may reflect lower vitality or hormonal imbalance and contribute to physiological decline. 

Either way, it’s a signal worth paying attention to.
