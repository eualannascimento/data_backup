“I’ll start when I feel motivated.”

Cool. See you never.

I trained with food poisoning last week. Not because I’m hardcore. Because I’m disciplined.

Motivation is a luxury. Discipline is a necessity.

The truth nobody wants to hear:

Your feelings don’t matter. Your commitment does.

Tired? Show up tired.
Unmotivated? Show up unmotivated.
Stressed? Show up stressed.
Sad? Show up sad.

The gym doesn’t care about your mood. Neither does success.

My worst workouts taught me more than my best ones.

They taught me I’m not controlled by feelings. I’m controlled by decisions.

And I decide to show up.

365 days of imperfect action beats 30 days of perfect motivation.

Your future self doesn’t care if you “felt like it.” They care that you did it.

Feelings are temporary. Results are permanent.

So my question to you today is:

What excuse died in your hands today?

- Dan
