A new 12-year study tracked over 3,600 adults to see how fast their bodies were aging using epigenetic clocks, and the results were clear.

Moderate exercise like brisk walking slowed aging. But the biggest impact came from vigorous activity, heavy lifting, and high-intensity intervals. These slowed the biological aging process the most across multiple aging markers.

Light activity? Barely moved the needle.

The takeaway: You don’t need to be an athlete to age well, but you do need to push past your comfort zone regularly.
