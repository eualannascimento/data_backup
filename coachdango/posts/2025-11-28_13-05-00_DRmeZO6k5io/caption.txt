Cardio is great for your heart. Terrible for fat loss.

Here’s why: Your body adapts fast. Week one burns 300 calories. Week eight? Maybe 150. Same effort, half the results.

Meanwhile, your appetite explodes. You burn 200 calories running, then eat 400 because “you earned it.”

Overdo it? You lose muscle. Metabolism tanks. Now you’re skinny-fat and exhausted.

The cardio trap: Once you start, you can’t stop. Miss a week? Weight returns instantly.

Better strategy: Lift weights. Fix nutrition. Walk daily.

It’s easier to skip 100 calories than burn them.

Use cardio for health. Use discipline for fat loss.
