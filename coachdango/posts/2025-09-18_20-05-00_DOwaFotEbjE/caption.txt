My client thought his life was over at 40.

“Too old to change,” he said. Accepted the dad bod. The low energy. The couch life.

Then his 65-year-old neighbor challenged him to a 5K.

The neighbor destroyed him.

That day, everything changed. Started lifting. Fixed his diet. Prioritized sleep.

18 months later:
- Ran his first marathon
- Deadlifting 400 lbs
- Dating a triathlete
- Started a new business

His neighbor? Still running circles around 40-year-olds.

“I wasn’t old,” he realized. “I was lazy.”

40 isn’t your expiration date. It’s your starting line.

So my question to you today is:

What excuse are you done with?

- Dan
