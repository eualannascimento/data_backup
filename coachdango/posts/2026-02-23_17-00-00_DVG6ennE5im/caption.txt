The harshest truth about getting in shape?

Lose weight as slowly as possible.

After my second kid, I felt wrecked.

Instead of a brutal 90-day cut, I gave myself a year.

That timeline changed everything.

Rushing fat loss triggers restriction, burnout, and rebound.

The weight comes off. Then comes right back.

Because the habits never stuck.

Slow it down. Build real systems. Let identity shift.

Better to lose 30 pounds in two years and keep it than lose it in six weeks and gain it back.

Speed kills sustainability.

Patience builds permanence.

What changes if you give yourself a year instead of six weeks?

- Dan
