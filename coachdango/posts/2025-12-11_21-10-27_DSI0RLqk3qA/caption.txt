For the past two months, I have stopped wearing a smartwatch, and it has changed my life.

Previously, I started off on an Apple Watch, then moved on to a Whoop band (and Oura), and finally graduated to a Garmin to track my cardiovascular activity.

But something I realized at some point is that once you have the data and you know what moves the needle and what doesn’t, then the device becomes a nuisance.

It overtakes the true measure of fitness, which is how you feel.

I’m not against wearables.

If you want to get control of your health, it is necessary to wear it for a period of time (could be 6 months, could be a year, could be 2 years).

But at some point in time, we must transcend them and not rely on a screen to tell us how we’re supposed to feel.

What about you? Agree or disagree?
