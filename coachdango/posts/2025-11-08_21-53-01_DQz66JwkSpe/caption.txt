The best drug on the planet is exercise, and it’s not even close.

My client Tom was taking 8 different medications at 52. Antidepressants, blood pressure pills, statins, sleep aids—his medicine cabinet looked like a pharmacy.

“Doc says I need these to function,” he told me.

I prescribed something different: 45 minutes of movement daily.

He laughed. “That’s it?”

6 months later, he’s down to 2 medications. His doctor is stunned. His wife says he’s a different man.

Exercise outperforms every pharmaceutical for mood, energy, and longevity.

Yet it sits there, free and available, while we pop pills.

So my question to you today is:

What medication could movement replace for you?
