In 2024, I decided to give up alcohol for six weeks, and I got so much benefit out of it that I decided to do it for a full year, and here’s what I learned...

I don’t think alcohol, like many people say, is a complete negative for your life.

I believe that it’s great for bonding and going deeper as a result of releasing your inhibitions.

But that’s about 10% of the alcohol equation. The other 90% is way different.

From a health perspective, there is zero healthy amount of alcohol that you can put into your body, and it has been linked to an abundance of diseases.

There is also the aspect of turning to something like alcohol to numb an emotion or to numb stress for the short term, only for that stress to come back even worse.

The way I like to think about drinking alcohol, especially if you take in excessive amounts, is that you are giving up 2-4 days of potential (energy, clarity, stress and anxiety relief) for one night of drinking.

Would I want to make that trade every week of my life? Absolutely not.

Does this mean I’m going to turn into a “”Teetotaler”” and shout from the top of a mountain and tell everyone to stop drinking alcohol? No.

I just want to encourage you to figure out what relationship you have to alcohol and what place it has in your life, and to be honest with yourself.

After a year of not drinking alcohol, I have essentially reduced my intake by about 99%.

If you’ve never given yourself a break from this substance, I suggest giving yourself a chance. You might be surprised at what you find out.

- Dan
