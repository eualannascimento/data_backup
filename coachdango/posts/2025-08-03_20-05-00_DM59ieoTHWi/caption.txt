I became a dad at 40.

That changed everything.

If I live to 80, I get 40 years with my kids.

Their weddings? I’ll be 65.
Their kids? I’ll be pushing 70.
My grandkids’ graduation? Do the math.

Most dads accept this. I refuse.

Every workout, every good meal, every night of sleep is an investment in time with them.

Because they’ll need their dad at 30.
And 40.
And 50.

I’m not checking out early.

So my question to you today is:

What’s your reason for wanting to stick around?

- Dan
