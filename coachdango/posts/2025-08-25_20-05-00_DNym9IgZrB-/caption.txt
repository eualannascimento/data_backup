Yesterday I watched my client run up the stairs.

Six months ago, he couldn’t walk up 5 steps without gasping.

He didn’t feel like showing up that day. Or most days.

But he showed up anyway.

That’s the difference between him and everyone still waiting to “feel motivated.”

I’ve trained hundreds of entrepreneurs. The successful ones all share one trait:

They show up when they don’t feel like it.

Especially when they don’t feel like it.

Your feelings are terrible, coaches.

They’ll tell you to skip when you need to train the most.
They’ll tell you to quit when you’re about to break through.
They’ll tell you tomorrow when today is all you have.

My client?

He’s shown up 180 days straight.

Maybe 10 of those days, he “felt like it.”

The other 170? Pure discipline.

Now he’s down 40 pounds.
Running his first 5K next month.
Off blood pressure meds.

All because he stopped negotiating with his feelings.

Your workout doesn’t care how you feel.
Neither does your body.

Show up anyway.

So my question to you today is:

How many days straight have you shown up?

- Dan
