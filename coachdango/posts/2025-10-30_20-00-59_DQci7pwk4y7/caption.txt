Scientists reversed 20 years of heart aging with a 2-year exercise protocol.

The study from UT Southwestern is groundbreaking.

61 sedentary adults aged 53 (±5 years) followed a structured program:
• 4-5 days/week exercise
• Mix of high-intensity intervals and steady cardio
• Progressive training over 24 months

Results after 2 years:
- 18% increase in VO2 max
- Reduced left ventricular stiffness
- Heart function equivalent to 30-year-old
- 88% adherence rate

The key? Starting in middle age (45-64) when hearts retain plasticity. After 65, the window closes.

Protocol included weekly:
• 1 high-intensity interval session
• 1 long aerobic session (60+ min)
• 2-3 moderate sessions
• 2 strength training days
