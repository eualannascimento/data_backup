Gratitude isn’t just feel-good fluff, it’s medicine.

Studies show practicing gratitude:
• Lowers inflammation markers by 23%
• Reduces cortisol (stress hormone)
• Improves sleep quality
• Strengthens immune function
• Decreases blood pressure

The mechanism? Gratitude activates your parasympathetic nervous system, your body’s “rest and repair” mode.

One study found that people who kept gratitude journals for 10 weeks had 25% better sleep and exercised 33% more.

It literally rewires your brain for resilience.

5 minutes each morning. Write 3 things you’re grateful for.

Your body will thank you.

So my question to you today is:

What are you grateful for today?
