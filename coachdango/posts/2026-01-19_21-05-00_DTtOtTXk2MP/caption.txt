If you’re gaining weight after 40, the problem isn’t your diet.

It’s not your genetics either.

It’s your identity.

You already know what to do. Eat better. Move more. Sleep enough.

The real question is: Who do you have to become for it to stick?

Tactics don’t fail. Identity does.

The person who “tries to eat healthy” will always lose to the person who says, “I’m someone who takes care of my body.”

One is a goal. The other is a lifestyle.

Stop chasing quick fixes. Start building the identity of a healthy person.

The results will follow.

Who do you need to become to hit your health goals?
