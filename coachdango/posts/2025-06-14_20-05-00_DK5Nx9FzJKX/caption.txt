The past week I’ve been moving homes and haven’t had time to workout.

Not being one for excuses, I made sure to do what I could, so I started using “Microworkouts”.

A Microworkout is short low to high-intensity exercises that helps you get your fitness in throughout the day.

Microworkouts work because some days you don’t have enough time or energy to get in the gym.

A study published in the European Journal of Applied Physiology had sedentary young adults do 20 second sprints where they pedaled as hard as they could.

They did these microworkouts 3x a day & after 6 weeks their cardiorespiratory fitness improved by 9%.

Another study published in Medicine & Science in Sports & Exercise found inactive yet healthy women improved cardiorespiratory fitness by doing 20 seconds of vigorous stair climbing 3x a day for three weeks.

While these are not a substitute for your normal training program they are a great way to get some fitness in without committing to a gym.

The next time you’re pressed for time and can’t make the gym try these out.

Doing something is always better than doing nothing.

So my question to you today is:

What was the last tiny habit that made a big difference?
