Back in my 20’s, I was a fat, directionless, high school dropout who sat at home and played video games all day, and at night did drugs and binge drank.

The one thing that turned my life around was being consistent with the gym.

I made a promise to myself to show up at the gym every single day. I didn’t have to do a workout. I didn’t even have to touch a weight. If I went to the gym, that was the only thing that mattered.

Lo and behold, when I got there, I told myself that I might as well do something while I’m here. And as one of my friends, Ed Latimore, would say, “The hardest weight to lift is the doors to the gym.”

So stop chasing shortcuts and start chasing consistency.

When all else fails, just show up.

- Dan
