I watched my 68-year-old client sprint yesterday.

His friends think he’s having a midlife crisis.
His doctor thinks he’s crazy.
His kids think he’s embarrassing.

What they don’t see:
Last year, he needed help getting off the couch.

The moment you stop moving like a human…
You start dying like one.

Society says “slow down.”
He said, “screw that.”

Your body doesn’t know age, only effort.
Stop asking, it stops responding.

He chose to sprint at 68 so he could still walk strongly at 78.

Age is inevitable.
Decay is optional.

Move like your life depends on it.
Because it does.

What movement did you give up that you’re ready to reclaim?

- Dan
