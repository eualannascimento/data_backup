The other day, I posted on X about not doing back squats or deadlifts anymore, and some people got mad like I was talking down on their religion.

But I still stand by that statement.

Are there people over 40 who do well with conventional deadlifts and back squats? Absolutely.

Should everyone have these exercises as a mandatory part of their workout program? Absolutely not.

The movements are mandatory, but not the exercises.

So if you find that the risk-reward is a little too much for you, especially after the age of 40, then try using some of the substitutions that I have included in the thread.

And if you are over the age of 40 and you still love doing squats and deadlifts, by all means, continue doing them.

The goal is to be able to lift for your entire lifetime, and whatever you think will help you get there, keep doing it.
