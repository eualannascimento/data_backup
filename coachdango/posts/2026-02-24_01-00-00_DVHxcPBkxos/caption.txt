I was at a business mastermind and eating with some of the attendees at a restaurant.

While everyone ordered burgers, fries & beers I opted to get a half chicken with potatoes, added some vegetables to the order and asked for a glass of water.

Some comments I heard was,

“𝘈𝘳𝘦 𝘺𝘰𝘶 𝘰𝘯 𝘢 𝘥𝘪𝘦𝘵?”⁣
“𝘊’𝘮𝘰𝘯 𝘋𝘢𝘯 𝘭𝘪𝘷𝘦 𝘢 𝘭𝘪𝘵𝘵𝘭𝘦”⁣
“𝘠𝘰𝘶’𝘳𝘦 𝘮𝘢𝘬𝘪𝘯𝘨 𝘶𝘴 𝘭𝘰𝘰𝘬 𝘣𝘢𝘥 𝘩𝘦𝘳𝘦”

I found it funny that eating this way was considered “dieting” and that living it up meant to order burger, fries and beer.

I’m not against those things at all either. I’m a fan of non restrictive diets.

But we’ve gotten to a point where eating chicken, veggies and potatoes look like you’re dieting…and if you think about it that’s weird.

Here’s the thing…

Every time you eat out you don’t have to eat like crap.

You don’t have to take it as an opportunity to go HAM with your diet.

A great form of self-mastery (if you’re into that thing) is the ability to make good choices regardless of your situation or how you feel.

Also, think farther than the meal in front of you.

I didn’t choose the burger, fries & beer because I had another 5 hours of masteminding ahead of me. I wouldn’t be able to think straight if I ate that food.

It’s okay to eat these foods, and it’s also okay to give yourself permission to make the right choice and delay gratification.

And avoid falling prey to the standards society has set for what makes a diet nowadays.

- Dan
