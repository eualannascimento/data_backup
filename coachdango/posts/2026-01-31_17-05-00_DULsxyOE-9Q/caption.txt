Turning 40 doesn’t mean you’re old, it means your lifestyle needs an upgrade.

Most people who feel “old” at 40 don’t have a metabolism problem. 

They have a habit problem. 

Not lifting weights. Drinking regularly. Takeout every day. Poor sleep. 

These are the things that slow you down, not age.

After 30, we lose 3–8% of muscle per decade. Strength training and nutrition become essential.

The good news? You can reverse the damage at any age.

The bad news? The longer you wait, the harder it gets.

“I’d rather be the oldest person in the gym than the youngest in a nursing home.”
