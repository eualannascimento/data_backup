Working out to look good is overrated. 
 
Working out to increase energy and improve brain function is underrated. 
 
There are 3 ways exercise helps your mind: 
 
1. Exercise feeds the brain 
 
Working out increases blood flow to the brain because of metabolic demand. 
 
The brain demands good circulation, and exercise aids in this by delivering nutrients to certain areas of the brain. 
 
2. Exercise secures memory 
 
Physical activity improves your memory strength and capacity. 
 
It does this by increasing Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor (BDNF). 
 
BDNF is known as the fertilizer of the brain. 
 
BDNF helps form new synapses that mediate memory and learning, making it easier to keep long-term memory. 
 
3. Exercise lowers stress 
 
When you work out, you decrease the number of stress receptors in the hippocampus. This minimizes the effect of stress hormones in the brain. 
 
You also release endorphins that create the “runner’s high”. This is why you feel amazing after a workout. They’ve also been shown to decrease anxiety and depression. 
 
If there was one health habit I’d start with, it would be exercise. 
 
It helps develop discipline, improves brain function, and makes you an overall healthy human being. 
 
So if you’re not working out, get started now. 
 
If not, you’re leaving some serious brain gains on the table.
