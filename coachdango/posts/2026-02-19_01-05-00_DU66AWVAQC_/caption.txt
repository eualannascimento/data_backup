My client couldn’t figure out why his knees were shot at 45. Then I showed him the math:

He was carrying 30 extra pounds.

That’s 120 pounds crushing his knees with every step.

180 pounds hammering his hips.

“But it’s only 30 pounds,” he said.

30 pounds x 10,000 steps = 1.2 million pounds of extra force. Daily.

His joints weren’t weak. They were being demolished.

6 months later: Down 35 pounds. Zero knee pain.

His orthopedic surgeon canceled his surgery.

The math doesn’t lie. Neither does your body.

How much extra weight are your joints carrying?
