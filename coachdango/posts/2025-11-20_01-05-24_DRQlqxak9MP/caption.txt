According to studies in JAMA the sedentary lifestyle of a desk job is as dangerous as smoking a cigarette.

Sitting 8+ hours a day slows metabolism and impairs blood sugar regulation, which can lead to diabetes and heart disease.

To counteract this, stand or walk every 30–60 minutes, aim for 7,000–10,000 steps daily, and incorporate strength training 2–3 times per week.

A standing desk or walking meetings can also help reverse the damage.

Movement is no longer optional, it’s medicine.
