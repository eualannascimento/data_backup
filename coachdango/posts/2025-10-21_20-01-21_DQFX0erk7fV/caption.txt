When I was younger, I would use food as a way to numb my emotions.

Feeling sad? Eat food.
Feeling happy? Eat food.
Feeling stressed? Eat food.

Something I realized is that eating food to numb an emotion gives you a short-term respite, but the emotion always comes back harder and stronger.

To fix this, I stopped using food and started using exercise to manage my emotions.

The key point is “manage.”

When I exercise and exhaust my body, it makes me easier to deal with life’s ups and downs.

This doesn’t mean my emotions are resolved; it just makes it easier to deal with.

The next time you’re feeling a strong emotion and you feel like eating or drinking, remind yourself that it’s better to exhaust the body to tame the mind.

- Dan
