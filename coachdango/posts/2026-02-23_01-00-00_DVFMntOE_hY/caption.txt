I had a conversation with one of the top sleep doctors in the United States.

We were talking about sleep cycles, and I asked him if they change as we get older.

He said that they do, but it’s also a matter of becoming wiser because the older you get, the more you prioritize your health.

Truth is, when you get your body in shape, it becomes its own alarm clock.

This is because of your circadian rhythms.

A circadian rhythm is your body’s natural way of operating. It dictates your sleep/wake cycles, your hormones, as well as behavioral changes during a 24-hour cycle.

What you should know is that the more you align with your rhythms, the better and more energetic you’ll feel.

Not being aligned with your circadian rhythms has been shown to reduce focus, attention, energy & memory. It’s also been associated with weight gain.

The best way to fix your rhythms is to fix your sleep, nutrition, and exercise habits.

Get the right amount of sleep and make sure to stay away from screens (or wear blue light blockers) at night.

Aim to exercise early in the day or in the afternoon. Try to not do it right before going to sleep.

Eat foods on a schedule, with your last meal coming at least 3 hours before sleeping.

I didn’t figure all this stuff out at one time. This stuff takes patience, so figure out one at a time and be easy on yourself.

Do you feel aligned with your rhythms?

If not, focus on one aspect that will help you align with them and take your time with it.

Wisdom is keeping one eye on the future while keeping your actions rooted in the present.
