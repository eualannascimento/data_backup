I talk a lot about creatine, but here’s who should not be taking it...

Despite a lack of evidence, people who have pre-existing kidney problems or kidney disease should err on the safe side and avoid taking creatine.

Creatine can cause increased kidney stress and also short-term elevation of creatinine levels.

Also, due to the fact that creatine helps you retain water, combined with other medications or dehydration, this can compromise vulnerable kidneys.

In general, there is limited research on the long-term safety of creatine in people with kidney disease, so this is why I recommend that they avoid it.

For everyone else, creatine is one of the most effective, cheapest supplements on the planet.

Note: When taking creatine, you will see a short-term increase in creatinine because it is a byproduct breakdown product of creatine. The rise has been shown to be harmless in healthy people and does not indicate kidney damage. It’s just a result of normal creatine metabolism.
