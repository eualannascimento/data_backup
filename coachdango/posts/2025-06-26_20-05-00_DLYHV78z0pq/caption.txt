People are going to get mad at me for saying this, but I’m going to say it anyways...

Most of the time, when someone is not losing weight and they say they’re in a calorie deficit, they are not in a calorie deficit.

Studies suggest that most people underreport their calorie intake by a substantial margin, sometimes by 30-55%.

Yes, things like thyroid can have an effect on this, but that represents a small percentage of the population.

Now, this doesn’t mean that hormones aren’t an issue.

Chronic stress can increase cortisol, which can increase our appetite and also add to belly fat.

Hunger hormones like ghrelin and leptin can be dysfunctional, leading to people eating more and not feeling full.

Hormones have an effect on weight loss, but it’s not what most people think.

Lastly, this is not taking into account metabolic adaptations and also damage. When people do low-calorie diets over the course of time while burning their hard-earned muscle, it will drive down their metabolism, which will cause them to lose less weight. 

But again, it still comes down to calories and calories out. 

I’ve yet to see one person defy the laws of thermodynamics.
