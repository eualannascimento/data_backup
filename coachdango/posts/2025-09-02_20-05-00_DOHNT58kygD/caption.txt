Your workout burns 300 calories. Your fork controls 3,000.

Stop trying to out-train your diet. You’ll lose every time.

I watched a client do 2-hour workouts.
Cardio. Weights. More cardio.
Still gained weight.

Why?

He’d “reward” himself with a smoothie bowl. 900 calories of sugar disguised as health.

3x the calories he just burned in 5 minutes.

Here’s the uncomfortable math:

That pizza? 45 minutes on the treadmill.
That craft beer? 20 minutes of burpees.
That “healthy” muffin? 30 minutes of rowing.

You can’t outrun your fork.

Use workouts for what they’re for:
• Building muscle
• Getting stronger
• Improving health
• Boosting mood

Use your diet for fat loss.

The gym builds your body. The kitchen reveals it.

Train to be strong. Eat to be lean.

Stop punishing yourself with exercise. Start nourishing yourself with food.

Your abs are made in the kitchen. Your strength is built in the gym.

Get the order right.

What’s your biggest diet vs workout misconception?
